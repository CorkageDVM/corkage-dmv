The Smart Diner's Guide

Maximize Your $400 Amex Platinum Resy Credit at BYOB Restaurants

Save on the wine by bringing your own bottle. Save on the food with your Amex dining credit. Here's how to do both at the same table across the DMV.

There are two ways a restaurant meal quietly drains your wallet: the wine markup and the check itself. Corkage solves the first — you bring a bottle you love and skip the 200–300% restaurant markup. The American Express Platinum Card's Resy dining credit can soften the second. Put them together and a special dinner gets dramatically more affordable, without compromising on the bottle or the table.

How the Amex Platinum Resy Credit Works

If you carry the American Express Platinum Card, you're entitled to a Resy dining credit that, used fully, returns up to $400 per year. The mechanics matter, so here they are clearly:

  • Up to $100 back each calendar quarter — that's four separate $100 windows (Jan–Mar, Apr–Jun, Jul–Sep, Oct–Dec), totaling up to $400 a year.
  • U.S. Resy restaurants only. The charge has to be at a restaurant that takes reservations through Resy and accepts the card.
  • Enrollment is required. Log into your Amex account and add the benefit before you rely on it.
  • Use it or lose it. The quarterly credit does not roll over. An unused Q2 $100 is simply gone on July 1.

Why BYOB Restaurants Are the Sweet Spot

The credit covers your restaurant charge — but it does nothing about an inflated wine list. That's exactly where corkage changes the math. When you dine at a BYOB-friendly restaurant:

  • You bring a bottle you already own (or bought at retail), avoiding the restaurant's markup entirely — often a $40–$120 swing on a single bottle.
  • You pay a modest corkage fee instead — typically $25–$40 per bottle across the DMV.
  • Your food and corkage charge run through the Platinum Card, working toward that $100 quarterly credit.

The result: you've trimmed the wine cost down to retail-plus-corkage, and Amex has effectively covered up to $100 of what's left. For a celebration dinner with a bottle that means something to you, it's hard to beat.

The Play, Step by Step

  1. Confirm the corkage policy. Use the Corkage DMV directory to find a restaurant that welcomes your bottle, and note the fee. Always call ahead — policies change.
  2. Check that it's on Resy. Search the restaurant in the Resy app and book your table there. After August 1, 2026, look for the credit-eligible badge.
  3. Bring the right bottle. Pick something that isn't already on the restaurant's wine list, and come prepared — see our What to Bring When You BYOB guide for the small kit that makes it effortless.
  4. Pay with your enrolled Platinum Card. Cover the meal and corkage on the card to trigger the statement credit.
  5. Repeat each quarter. Four dinners a year, one per quarter, is all it takes to capture the full $400.

What's Changing This Summer

In February 2026, American Express announced it's folding Tock into Resy, combining both platforms under Resy over the summer. The merge roughly doubles Resy to 25,000+ venues — adding a wave of fine-dining restaurants and wineries that previously lived only on Tock. For BYOB diners, that's a meaningful expansion: many serious, cellar-friendly restaurants are migrating in, and Tock venues will begin becoming Resy-credit-eligible this summer (with the majority eligible by the end of 2027). In short, the list of places where you can stack corkage savings with your Amex credit is about to get a lot longer.

A Few Honest Caveats

Bringing your own bottle is a privilege, not a hack. To keep it gracious:

  • Order a meal — corkage exists for diners, not for drinking at the bar.
  • Tip on the full experience. Your server still opens, decants, and pours your wine; tip as though you'd ordered a comparable bottle from their list.
  • Don't bring a bottle that's on their list. It's the one near-universal corkage rule.
