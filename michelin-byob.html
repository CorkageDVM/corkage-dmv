The Ultimate BYOB

Bring Your Own Bottle to a Michelin-Starred Table

A rare few of Washington's starred kitchens will pour the bottle you carry in. It's the most special — and most expensive — way to BYOB in the DMV.

Opening a cellar bottle at a Michelin-starred tasting counter is a particular kind of thrill — and a particular kind of responsibility. Corkage here runs steep, the service is exacting, and the "your wine can't be on our list" rule carries real weight when the list is this deep. Bring something extraordinary, tip generously, and let the sommelier guide the pour. As of mid-2026, these three DC stars allow it.

The Short List Is Short for a Reason

Most of DC's most celebrated kitchens do not allow BYOB — Rose's Luxury, The Dabney, and Little Pearl among them — because their own programs are central to the experience. That's exactly why the three above stand out. And remember: at a starred restaurant, the bottle you bring should be something genuinely rare or personal, not a label they'd happily sell you.
