Tock Is Folding Into Resy This Summer — Here's What It Means for BYOB Diners

American Express is merging its two reservation platforms into one. For people who bring their own wine, the change quietly works in your favor.

Published June 2026 · Corkage DMV

If you've ever booked a tasting menu or a winery dinner online, you've probably used Tock. Soon you won't — at least not by that name. American Express is folding Tock into Resy this summer, combining both reservation platforms under the Resy brand. The dining press is covering it as a reservation-wars story. But there's a smaller, more interesting angle that most coverage is skipping: what it means for diners who bring their own wine.

What's Actually Happening

In February 2026, American Express — which owns Resy — announced it would merge Resy with Tock, the reservation platform it acquired and best known for prepaid bookings, tasting menus, wineries, and fine dining. Over the course of the summer, the two are being combined into a single platform under Resy. The headline numbers:

  • The merge roughly doubles Resy to more than 25,000 venues, bringing Tock's fine-dining restaurants and wineries onto Resy for the first time.
  • The Tock app and website will eventually shut down, with restaurants migrating into Resy instead.
  • Resy gains some of Tock's signature features along the way, including tiered experiences and prepaid bookings.

For Amex, the goal is scale — a single platform large enough to take on OpenTable, which still lists far more restaurants worldwide. For diners, the practical upshot is that a whole category of serious, cellar-minded restaurants is about to live in one app.

Why BYOB Diners Should Care

Here's the connection the dining headlines miss. The restaurants that historically lived on Tock — chef-driven tasting destinations, special-occasion rooms, and especially wineries — skew heavily toward exactly the kind of place where a thoughtful corkage program lives. These are venues built around the wine experience, not around moving bottles off a markup-heavy list.

As those venues migrate onto Resy, two things happen for someone who likes to bring a special bottle:

  • Discovery gets easier. More cellar-friendly restaurants in one place means a better shot at finding a table that welcomes your wine.
  • The Amex Resy credit reaches more of them. This is the big one — see below.
The Credit Angle

The American Express Platinum Card's Resy dining credit returns up to $100 per quarter ($400 a year) at U.S. Resy restaurants. Until now, many fine-dining spots booked through Tock or OpenTable, so they didn't qualify. As Tock venues move onto Resy, they begin becoming credit-eligible this summer, with the majority expected to qualify by the end of 2027. Translation: the list of places where you can bring your own bottle and offset the meal with your Amex credit is about to grow considerably. What It Means Here in the DMV

Locally, the shift is already taking shape. Aventino Cucina in Bethesda ($40 corkage, two-bottle max) is on Resy today and lets you stack corkage savings with the Amex credit. As more area fine-dining rooms and nearby wineries migrate from Tock to Resy over the summer, expect that list to lengthen — and we'll keep the directory updated as it does.

What to Do Right Now

  • If you have an upcoming Tock reservation, hold onto your confirmation and watch for migration notices — your booking and any prepayment should carry over, but confirm with the restaurant.
  • If you carry an Amex Platinum, make sure your Resy credit is enrolled, and start checking the Resy app for the credit-eligible badge (required at checkout as of August 1, 2026).
  • Either way, keep doing the simple thing that saves the most: bring a bottle you love to a restaurant that welcomes it.
