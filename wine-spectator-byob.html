Where the Cellar Meets the Corkage

Wine Spectator Award Restaurants That Welcome Your Bottle

A handful of the DMV's most celebrated wine destinations also let you bring your own. When the list is this good, the bottle you carry in had better be worth it.

A Wine Spectator award is shorthand for a restaurant that takes wine seriously — a deep, thoughtfully built list and a team that knows it. There's a lovely tension in bringing your own bottle to one of these rooms: corkage rules say your wine can't already be on their list, and at a cellar this deep, that means you're bringing something genuinely special — a back-vintage, a gift, a label they don't carry. Here are the award winners in our directory that welcome exactly that.

What the Awards Mean

Wine Spectator's three tiers

Award of Excellence

A well-chosen list — typically 90+ selections — with thematic match to the menu. The entry tier, and still a strong signal.

Best of Award of Excellence

A serious cellar — usually 350+ selections with real vintage depth across major regions. The step up most of this list occupies.

Grand Award

The pinnacle — only a few dozen worldwide. In the DMV, Fiola in DC holds the lone Grand Award — though it does not permit BYOB, so come for the cellar, not the corkage.
